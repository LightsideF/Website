We take over the debt matter entirely. Your client’s relationship with you remains intact and undisturbed. We keep you informed of progress at whatever level of detail is useful, and we work to a conclusion — not just an initial assessment.

Questions people often ask

Yes — and this is actually the best time to get advice. Most people who contact us are still paying, but watching the balance refuse to move. The payments are going in but the debt isn’t shrinking, and the situation is slowly getting worse rather than staying the same. Getting advice early means more options are available and more can often be achieved. You do not need to have missed a payment or received a formal demand before speaking to us.

For most people, unsecured debts — credit cards, personal loans, overdrafts — are not directly secured against your home. A creditor cannot simply take your home because you have stopped paying a credit card. However, if a creditor obtains a County Court Judgment and then applies for a charging order, they can seek to secure that debt against your property. This is not automatic and does not happen quickly, but it is a risk that exists if debts are left unmanaged over time. One of the first things we establish in any initial conversation is the position of the family home — and in the majority of cases, it is not at risk in the way people fear.

You can, and some people do. Writing to creditors, requesting hardship arrangements, and negotiating directly are all possible. The difficulty is that creditors deal with debt advisers every day and know what is and is not achievable. An experienced adviser will often secure better terms, get interest and charges frozen more reliably, and handle the process in a way that protects your legal position. More practically — dealing with creditors yourself takes time, causes stress, and requires a level of knowledge about what you are entitled to ask for that most people simply don’t have. That is not a criticism; it is just not something most people have ever had to learn. It is precisely what we do.
