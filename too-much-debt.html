Understanding your situation

Debt doesn't have to reach a crisis point before you can get help

“Too much debt” isn’t one situation — it’s a phrase people use when the numbers have stopped working and they can’t see a way through. If any of the following feels familiar, you are in the right place.

Does any of this feel familiar?

  • You make monthly payments but the total keeps growing, not shrinking
  • You are using one debt to pay another
  • A life event has changed your financial position and the old approach no longer holds
  • A creditor is threatening legal action, a CCJ, or has appointed a debt collector
  • You can see no realistic way to repay what you owe within a reasonable timeframe
  • You have business debt that is personally guaranteed

If you recognised yourself in any of these, you are in the right place. These are not unusual situations — they are the ones we work with every day.

The difference we make

Referring a client

When your client's debt has become the problem you can't solve for them

Solicitors, accountants, and mortgage advisers refer clients to Lightside when a debt situation has developed beyond what they can address in their own practice. The referral is usually triggered by one of a handful of recognisable situations — and if any of these are familiar, an initial discussion costs nothing and is completely confidential.

We take over the debt matter entirely. Your client's relationship with you remains intact and undisturbed. We keep you informed of progress at whatever level of detail is useful, and we work to a conclusion — not just an initial assessment. You will always know who at Lightside is handling the matter and can reach them directly.

Lightside is FCA-authorised and a recognised specialist in complex personal and business debt, equipped to handle situations that fall outside the scope of generic debt management services. Most of the cases on this page were referred by professionals: solicitors managing divorce proceedings, accountants whose clients' businesses had closed, and mortgage advisers blocked from progressing a remortgage because of the client's existing unsecured debt.

Debt blocking a transaction or process A remortgage declined because of unsecured debt, a divorce settlement stalled by joint or sole liabilities, a business sale complicated by personal guarantees — we have navigated all of these.
A client in distress, not yet at formal insolvency Many of the best outcomes happen before a formal process becomes necessary. Early referral almost always produces more options, not fewer. Do not wait for a statutory demand or bankruptcy petition.
Mixed personal and business debt Personal guarantees called in, director liability, business closure leaving personal creditors — situations that cross the personal/business boundary need a coordinated approach across both.
Home or property at risk of enforcement Charging orders, bankruptcy petitions, or threatened CCJ enforcement where property is involved. We can often find alternatives to formal insolvency — but acting early is critical.
A client who needs creditors handled for them Once we are instructed, all creditor contact is redirected to Lightside. Your client stops receiving calls, letters, and debt collection agency contact — immediately, not eventually.

Questions people often ask

The payments are going in but the debt isn’t shrinking, and the situation is slowly getting worse rather than staying the same. Getting advice early means more options are available and more can often be achieved. You do not need to have missed a payment or received a formal demand before speaking to us.

For most people, unsecured debts — credit cards, personal loans, overdrafts — are not directly secured against your home. A creditor cannot simply take your home because you have stopped paying a credit card. However, if a creditor obtains a County Court Judgment and then applies for a charging order, they can seek to secure that debt against your property. This is not automatic and does not happen quickly, but it is a risk that exists if debts are left unmanaged over time. One of the first things we establish in any initial conversation is the position of the family home — and in the majority of cases, it is not at risk in the way people fear.

You can, and some people do. Writing to creditors, requesting hardship arrangements, and negotiating directly are all possible. The difficulty is that creditors deal with debt advisers every day and know what is and is not achievable. An experienced adviser will often secure better terms, get interest and charges frozen more reliably, and handle the process in a way that protects your legal position. More practically — dealing with creditors yourself takes time, causes stress, and requires a level of knowledge about what you are entitled to ask for that most people simply don’t have. That is not a criticism; it is just not something most people have ever had to learn. It is precisely what we do.
